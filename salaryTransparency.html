Salary Transparency

Most women still makes 84¢ to a man’s dollar when working the same job, a fact which has held true for over twenty years.

We are able to tell a sucess story with the change toward more equal pay in regards to elderly people, going from $5,200 vs $26,900 annual earns in 1964, to 90% today.

Black, hispanic, and other women of color salaries average to 66% of that of mens. Part of this is due to less oppertunites for higher paying jobs.

A controversial, but easy way to combat this discontinuity in salaries is by having employees be transparent with each other about salaries. One may think that the amount they are making is what everyone is making, and not asking or discussing it is what allows it to fly under the radar.
